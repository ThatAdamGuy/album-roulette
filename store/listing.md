## Detailed description

(Plain text only — Chrome Web Store does NOT render Markdown. No bullets with markdown syntax; use plain dashes/line breaks as shown. Paste as-is.)

```
No music service lets you say "play me a random album, start to finish" — not Spotify, not Apple Music, not YouTube Music itself. Album Roulette fixes that for YouTube Music.

One click on the dice button in the YouTube Music header picks a random album from your library — including uploaded albums — and plays it track 1, in order, no shuffle. It avoids repeating your last 20 picks so you're not hearing the same handful of albums on rotation.

FEATURES

- One-click random album, start to finish, no shuffle
- Works with uploaded albums, not just streaming catalog albums
- Genre chips — albums are auto-tagged by genre in the background, so you can roll "Jazz," "Metal," or whatever's in your library specifically
- Semi-random "Deal me 6" — see six album covers pulled from six different genres and pick the one you're in the mood for
- Optional Keep Rolling mode — when an album ends, a short chime plays and the next random album starts automatically, so you can queue up a whole random listening session hands-free
- Keyboard shortcut (Alt+Shift+R) to roll from anywhere
- Right-click the toolbar icon for a quick genre menu
- A short onboarding walkthrough the first time you install it

PRIVACY

Album Roulette has no account system, no API keys to configure, and no developer-run server. There is no analytics or tracking of any kind. Everything the extension needs — your album list, your genre tags, your recent picks — is stored locally in your browser via chrome.storage.local and never leaves your device except for one narrow case: to label an album's genre, the extension sends that album's title and artist name to two public, keyless music databases (the Apple iTunes Search API and MusicBrainz) so it can look up the genre. That's the only data that ever leaves your browser, and it's limited to the album title and artist — nothing about you, your account, or your listening habits.

REQUIREMENTS

You'll need a YouTube Music account with at least a few albums saved to your library (playlists of full albums, or uploaded albums) for Album Roulette to have something to roll. It works on music.youtube.com in Chrome.

Album Roulette is an independent, unofficial project and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by YouTube, Google, or Apple.
```
